About

Hi, and welcome to my portfolio. My name is William Chatterson and I’m a junior in the University of Michigan’s School of Information (SI), an interdisciplinary program which focuses on the intersection of people, technology, and information. Within SI, I specialize in user experience (UX) & design. I see myself as action-oriented and as a collaborator. I especially love working with creative people, weaving together ideas, and producing something tangible to be proud of. Outside of that, I love culture- especially niche or foreign cultures. I really became fascinated with the concept after studying/living in Amman, Jordan for about four months (where I also developed advanced Arabic language skills). I see culture as being an aggregated reflection of individual’s choices, behavior, and identity, and I see UX & design as being a direct way reach and impact people by utilizing aspects of both culture and psychology.

I’m experienced with a number of UX methods including remote and in-person usability testing, comparative analysis, and heuristic evaluation. Down the page you will find tangible representation of my experience with both UX & design as well as my resume. However, I’d like to note that the first element of my portfolio is actually this website, which I built from scratch.

UX

Card Sorting

For this excercise, I had subjects use note cards to sort products from Sears' electronics section into categories which they found intuitive. The goal was to create a easily-navigable and intuitive site hierarchy.

Card Sorting: Results

After aggregating and analyzing the 7 or 8 sorting tests, I recommended this site hierarchy.

Personas

These personas were developed based on eight face-to-face interviews conducted by my team. The purpose of these personas is to create a working and predictable model of behavior for the average users of Mbus, an app which tracks public transit on my campus.

Logo Project

This picture is from a logo project I recently completed. The goal was to create an effective logo for Galaxy, a conference which brings together intellectuals and leaders from around the world to focus for a day on creating clean, sustainable solutions to modern problems. For this project I developed a main logo, color variations from that logo, and child logos which would serve as marks for the sub-conferences within the main event.
